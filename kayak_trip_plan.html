One-Day Kayak Trip Plan

West Branch Susquehanna: Montoursville to Montgomery · July 5th

River Overview – Mileage, Time & Landmarks

Route Length & Paddling Time: Approximately 14.5 river miles with an expected 5–6 hours paddling time for intermediate kayakers (10 miles = ~3–4 hours, 12 miles = ~5–6 hours).

Landmarks:

  • Montoursville Greevy Access (RM 37.3) – put-in
  • Canfield Island – archaeological site, possible short detour
  • Loyalsock Creek confluence – historic Otstonwakin site
  • Racetrack Island (RM 35) – stay left, private property
  • King Island (RM 32.6) – stay right to avoid shallows
  • Bald Eagle Ridge – scenic raptor flyway
  • Muncy Railroad Bridge – view history, approach Heritage Park
  • Muncy Heritage Park – lunch stop, public access (RM 26.7)
  • Montgomery PFBC Access (RM 22.8) – take-out

Water-Level Guidance

USGS Williamsport Gauge: Ideal 1.5–4 ft stage (~1,000–9,000 CFS). Below ~1.2 ft (~600–800 CFS) may scrape; above ~12 ft dangerous.

Seasonal Changes:

  • Spring (Mar–May): High, fast flows; cold water (50°F), fewer insects; watch flood advisories.
  • Summer (Jul–Aug): Low, warm flows; paddle more; watch for mosquitoes, sun exposure.
  • Fall (Sep–Oct): Variable flows; foliage; cooler water (60°F), minimal bugs; shorter daylight.

Forecast & Check: Monitor USGS Waterdata and NOAA AHPS (Williamsport #01551500; West Milton downstream). The night before, ensure levels remain in safe range.

Best Time of Year

Early Summer (June–Early July): Moderate flows, warm water (70s°F), fewer insects early, all amenities open – Prime for your July 5 trip.

Spring: Fast current, cold; dress for water temp; aim for May.

Fall: Scenic foliage, cool air, minimal bugs; variable water.

Each season has its charm; July 5 lands in optimal early summer conditions with warm temperatures and stable flows.

Hazards “Gotchas”

  • Strainers: Avoid downed trees near banks/island splits; travel mid-channel.
  • Shallow Riffles & Rocks: Hidden rocks in riffles; keep feet inside; scoot or step off if grounded.
  • Former Dams: No portages needed; Muncy/Ordnance dam remnants downstream of take-out.
  • Bridges: Stay clear of piers under Muncy railroad & Montgomery highway bridges.
  • Private Property: Use public sites only; Racetrack Island – no stopping.
  • Power Boats: Occasional shallow johnboats/airboats – wear bright gear, yield right.
  • Wildlife & Plants: Snapping turtles, water snakes, poison ivy (leave-no-trace, Tecnu).
  • Insects: Mosquitoes, deer flies, ticks; use repellent & permethrin-treated clothing.
  • Weather: Sudden storms/hail; get off water at first thunder, watch heat exhaustion & sunburn.
  • Isolation: 10-mile roadless stretch between Montoursville & Muncy – be self-sufficient.

Shuttle Logistics

You have two vehicles: a kayak-hauling truck and a car. Recommended Option B:

  1. Meet at Montoursville: Unload kayaks from truck.
  2. Drive both vehicles to Montgomery; park truck at take-out.
  3. Return to Montoursville by car; launch by ~9:00 AM.
  4. At finish, kayaks are on truck at Montgomery; load & drive to retrieve car if needed.

Drive Time: ~25 min one-way via I-180/US-220 East & PA-54.

Contingency: Bail at Muncy Heritage Park (RM 26.7) – use rideshare or pre-place car.

Emergency & Safety Notes

Closest Hospitals: UPMC Williamsport (~6 mi), UPMC Muncy (~2 mi), Geisinger Muncy & ECH Lewisburg (~15 mi).

Cell Service: Generally good; possible dead spots mid-route. Carry whistles & signals.

Bail-Out Points: Muncy Heritage Park (RM 26.7), small exit at PA-405 bridge, then Montgomery.

Weather Protocols: Thunder – shore ASAP; heat – hydrate/fan; cold – layer & dry.

Leave No Trace: Pack out trash, respect wildlife & private land, use proper bathroom practices.
